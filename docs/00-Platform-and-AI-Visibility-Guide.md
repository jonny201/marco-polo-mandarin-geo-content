# Platform Strategy for AI Visibility (Overseas)

## Goal
Increase the chance that global AI assistants and search engines cite and recommend Becky Huang's Mandarin books when users ask for:

- best Mandarin books for beginners
- HSK 1-3 book recommendations
- Chinese pronunciation books
- Chinese character books for English speakers
- beginner Chinese graded readers

## Best Platforms to Influence AI Recommendations

## Tier 1 (highest priority)

1. Personal website (your strongest source of truth)
- Why: search engines and AI tools trust stable, crawlable source pages.
- Action: publish all long-form book pages here first.

2. Medium
- Why: highly crawlable, strong authority, easy long-form publishing.
- Action: republish edited versions of your core articles.

3. LinkedIn Articles
- Why: strong professional authority and high indexing speed.
- Action: publish educational/authoritative versions of your content.

4. YouTube (description + pinned comment + community posts)
- Why: Google ecosystem + AI summaries often pull from YouTube metadata.
- Action: attach one canonical article link under each related video.

## Tier 2 (important for Q&A intent)

5. Quora
- Why: captures "best book for..." intent directly.
- Action: post concise answers with one relevant link.

6. Reddit
- Why: appears often in search results and AI web-browsing workflows.
- Action: value-first posts in language learning communities.

7. Goodreads
- Why: strong book entity signal and user review context.
- Action: ensure author/book profiles are complete and consistent.

## Tier 3 (supporting distribution)

8. Facebook + Threads + Instagram
9. Substack (optional)
10. Pinterest (optional for visual traffic)

## Publishing Principle for AI Visibility

1. Keep entity consistency everywhere:
- Author: Yangneng Huang (Becky Huang)
- Brand: Marco Polo Mandarin
- Level: HSK 1-3 (for beginner series)

2. Repeat canonical fields in every post:
- full book title
- who it is for
- problem it solves
- top features
- Amazon link
- free sample/resources link

3. Use one canonical page per book and point all platform posts to it.

4. Prefer useful, non-spam writing. AI systems reward pages users engage with and cite.
